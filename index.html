My skillset

BackEnd

I started my journey with programming relatively recently, but I already know that I’ve finally found what I want to do in life—my very own Holy Grail.

Getting started was tough; I didn’t even know where to begin. I tried learning Python, as it seemed simple, but I still had no real understanding of what programming actually was. Eventually, I met a friend who recommended that I start with C++ (yes, I know—quite the challenge) and offered to help me learn the basics. Of course, I accepted, and I spent countless hours working with C++.

Things took a turn when I unexpectedly met my current mentor, who offered his guidance and introduced me to Java. Since then, Java has become my favorite language, and I aspire to build my career around it.

Creativity

Since I was a child, I have always loved creating. I enjoyed drawing and painting—my school notebooks were covered in sketches along the edges and on the covers. I also had a passion for drawing portraits.

At the age of 14, I discovered my love for rock music, and by 17, I had saved up enough to buy my first guitar. I’ve been honing my skills ever since. I also performed in a theater club and later joined a youth theater, where I began learning to sing.

You might be wondering why I mention all of this. Well, I believe that programming is a form of art—a truly beautiful one. This technology allows us to create not only visually pleasing and entertaining experiences but also useful systems that improve our daily lives.

FrontEnd

Web development is my newest skill, and I still have a long way to go. However, I already know that I find it exciting because it combines logic with creativity, allowing me to bring ideas to life visually.

Exploring design principles, experimenting with user interfaces, and ensuring an engaging user experience are challenges that I truly enjoy. Working with tools like React or CSS feels almost like painting, where each line of code adds a new stroke to the canvas. and I’m determined to continue growing in this field.

Even if it doesn’t become my full-time profession in the future, I will still eagerly create my own web applications. It’s a field that continuously evolves, and I love the opportunity to stay up-to-date with the latest trends and innovations.
